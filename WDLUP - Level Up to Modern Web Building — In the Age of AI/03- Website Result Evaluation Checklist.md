# AI-Generated Website — Evaluation Checklist

Use this checklist after generating a website with an AI tool (v0, Bolt, Lovable, or similar).
It helps review the output in a structured way — catching what's easy to miss when the excitement of "it just built a website" is still fresh.

Go through each category. Check what looks correct, note what needs attention, and skip anything that doesn't apply to your project.

---

## 1. Intent Alignment

> Does the site communicate what it's supposed to?

These are the most important checks — a site can look great but still miss the point entirely.

- [ ] **Core message** — Would a first-time visitor understand what this site is about within a few seconds?
- [ ] **Primary CTA** — Is the main action clear, visible, and easy to find without scrolling too far?
- [ ] **Audience fit** — Does the tone and language feel right for the intended audience?
- [ ] **First impression** — Does the hero/opening section match the feeling you described? (e.g., professional, friendly, bold)

## 2. Content Accuracy

> Is the content correct, complete, and faithful to the original definition?

AI tools often generate plausible-sounding text that doesn't actually match what was described. Read carefully.

- [ ] **Names & terms** — Organization name, tagline, product/service names — all spelled correctly and used consistently?
- [ ] **Descriptions** — Do the texts accurately describe the project, mission, or offer? (not generic filler)
- [ ] **Section completeness** — Are all the sections you defined actually present?
- [ ] **Placeholder detection** — Any leftover placeholder text, "Lorem ipsum", or generic content that wasn't replaced?
- [ ] **Accuracy of claims** — Does the site say anything that isn't true or that the client didn't say?

## 3. Structure & Flow

> Does the page guide the visitor in a logical, natural way?

A good page tells a story: who you are, what you offer, why it matters, what to do next.

- [ ] **Section order** — Does the sequence make sense? Does each section lead naturally into the next?
- [ ] **Navigation** — If there's a menu or navigation links, do they work and point to the right places?
- [ ] **Story coherence** — Scrolling from top to bottom, does the page build a clear narrative?
- [ ] **No dead ends** — Is there always a next step visible? (No sections that leave the visitor with "now what?")

## 4. Visual Coherence

> Does the look and feel match the direction you gave?

You don't need to be a designer to evaluate this — trust your eyes and compare against what you asked for.

- [ ] **Color match** — Do the colors used reflect the palette or direction you described?
- [ ] **Typography feel** — Does the font choice feel appropriate for the tone? (e.g., clean and modern vs. formal and traditional)
- [ ] **Visual breathing room** — Is there enough whitespace, or does it feel cramped and overwhelming?
- [ ] **Image/media fit** — Do images or placeholders feel aligned with the overall style? (not random or mismatched)
- [ ] **Consistency** — Does the visual style stay consistent across all sections? (no sudden style shifts)

**Notes:**

---

## 5. Basic Usability

> Can a real person actually use this site comfortably?

No technical tools needed for this — just try using the site as a visitor would.

- [ ] **Readability** — Is all text easy to read? (no tiny fonts, no low-contrast text on busy backgrounds)
- [ ] **Mobile check** — Resize the browser window or check on your phone: does the layout still work?
- [ ] **Clickable elements** — Do buttons and links look clickable? Are they big enough to tap on mobile?
- [ ] **Loading feel** — Does the page feel clean and snappy, or heavy and cluttered?

**Notes:**

---

## 6. Client Readiness

> If building this for someone else — would they recognize their project?

This is the gut check. Everything above can pass, but if the client looks at it and says "that's not us," there's work to do.

- [ ] **Recognition test** — Would the client immediately see their project in this site?
- [ ] **Brand alignment** — Does it feel like *their* brand, not a generic template?
- [ ] **Nothing misleading** — Is there anything that could confuse or misrepresent the project?
- [ ] **Shareable as a draft** — Would you feel comfortable showing this as a "first draft" to start a conversation?

**Notes:**

---

## After the checklist

You should now have:
- A clear picture of what the AI got right
- A concrete list of things that need fixing or adjusting
- Confidence that you haven't missed anything obvious

> **Remember:** This is a first draft, not a final product. The goal isn't perfection — it's having a structured understanding of where things stand and what needs work next.
